Observing the knowledge retrieval problem

Google’s enterprise sales team was spending 4+ hours per week manually researching and drafting responses to repetitive client queries. The information existed internally — but was siloed across docs, slides, and email threads. The root problem wasn’t content quality; it was retrieval latency. Every hour spent searching was an hour not spent selling.

Scoping the pilot and defining success

A 50-person sales team was selected as the initial pilot segment — small enough to move fast, large enough to generate statistically meaningful signal. Success was defined as measurable weekly time savings per person, not just adoption. The core hypothesis: if an AI agent could retrieve and draft in under 30 seconds what took a human 20 minutes, the behaviour change would be self-sustaining.

Designing a fully autonomous Gmail agent

The system was designed as a fully autonomous loop: inbound email triggers the agent → Gemini reasons over an internal knowledge base using a Think tool → a contextual draft is generated → presented for human approval → sent. The critical design constraint was human-in-the-loop approval before sending — preserving trust while removing the research burden entirely.

Building and instrumenting the agent pipeline

Built the agent pipeline using Gemini, Gmail API, and an internal sales knowledge base. A key build decision was to instrument every interaction for behavioral data capture — not just for product improvement, but because this data was earmarked to feed Gemini’s internal personalization layer. Usage tracking was a first-class requirement, not an afterthought.

Piloting and scaling through word of mouth

Launched to the 50-person sales pilot with no formal announcement. Within two weeks, users were sharing the tool across the wider org without any prompting. The product grew not through a rollout plan but through word-of-mouth — a signal that time savings were immediately obvious and the tool was genuinely changing how people worked day-to-day.

60x scale, 4 hrs saved, and a Gemini contribution

The pilot scaled 60x from 50 to 3,000+ internal users, driven entirely by organic adoption. Each user saved an average of 4 hours per week — time previously spent on information retrieval. The behavioral data captured from this deployment was fed directly into Gemini’s internal personalization layer, making the project’s impact extend well beyond the tool itself.
